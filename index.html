Email Management SOPs

Email Management Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

This section outlines the standard operating procedures for managing Hillary's email inbox. The primary goal is to organize, prioritize, and respond to emails efficiently on her behalf, freeing up her time for more critical tasks.

1. Initial Setup and Daily Open Tabs

Upon starting your day, open the following five tabs:

  • Hillary’s Google Calendar
  • Your Google Calendar
  • Hillary’s Email Inbox
  • Your Email Inbox
  • Hello Email Inbox
  • Relaunch Email Inbox

Additionally, keep the main Slack channel and any other channels Hillary is a part of open for communication.

2. Daily Inbox Cleanup

The first task each morning is to categorize and clean up Hillary's inbox.

  • Email Lists (Spam): Identify emails from "email lists" (e.g., "Unsubscribe" button, no yellow checkmark). Apply the "Email List" label and archive them. Create a cheat sheet of common vendors/mass senders.
  • General Response Emails: For unsolicited emails from people wanting to work with Hillary, respond with a pre-written template (thank them, state Hillary is not interested). Respond on her behalf. Templates are in a shared Google Sheet.

3. Labeling and Organization

Labels are crucial for organization. Active and In Use Labels:

  • Email List: Mass emails, promotional content.
  • Debbie Allen: Communication with Debbie regarding events.
  • Fathom Meeting Recaps: File all Fathom recordings and notes.
  • Global Women’s Club: Correspondence related to this program.
  • One-to-One Coaching Clients: Has two sub-labels: Past and Present coaching clients.
  • Invoices Paid: Emails confirming invoice payment.
  • Catherine (Iconic): Emails from Catherine regarding the website.
  • Relaunch Team: For organizing emails from team members after Hillary has seen/responded.
  • Vendors: Organizing communications with vendors/connections after follow-up.
  • Urgent: Time-sensitive emails requiring immediate attention.
  • FYI: Emails Hillary should look at but are not urgent; often for your reference.

Inactive Labels: Create a parent label "Archive" and move all inactive labels (e.g., "Leave It Board") under it for historical reference without clutter.

4. Managing Connections and Fathom Meetings

  • Handling Connection Emails: Respond immediately to emails from connections (e.g., Danny Berman) using the template (thank connector & connected person, include Hillary's Google Calendar link). These are time-sensitive.
  • Processing Fathom Meeting Recaps: Immediately open Fathom recordings. Copy link to Iconic 100 spreadsheet with name, email, next steps. Listen to the last five minutes for action steps. Send follow-up email on Hillary's behalf using a template, summarizing call and next steps.
  • Meeting Preparation: At least a week in advance, fill calendar event sidebar with connection source, brief summary. Find and paste LinkedIn profile if not provided. Include previous Fathom summary if Hillary has met the person before.

5. Communication and Reporting

  • End-of-Day Text: Send Hillary a text recapping completed tasks (emails responded to, meetings scheduled, urgent items).
  • Urgent Emails: Reiterate any "Urgent" emails in your end-of-day text.
  • Early-Morning Urgent Emails: If an urgent email arrives in the morning and you don't know how to respond, text Hillary immediately for guidance.
  • During Travel: Be extra diligent in your email and Slack when Hillary is traveling, as she may send new tasks.

Calendar Management SOPs

Calendar Management Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

1. Color-Coding System

A consistent color-coding system is used to categorize events on Hillary's calendar. Apply the appropriate color to each new event. Note that Hillary may change this system, so communicate with her to stay updated.

  • Red: Workouts
  • Yellow: Operational Meetings (e.g., team meetings, one-on-one meetings with Kristen, Patty, you, etc.)
  • Blue (Impact Millionaire calls): Connect calls from external partners (e.g., Danny Burmank, Catherine, Jennifer Fry, etc.)
  • Pink: Personal appointments and travel (e.g., flights, appointments, airport travel)
  • Light Green: Revenue-driving events and workshops (e.g., bi-weekly webinars, LinkedIn events)
  • Light Blue (Kid Time): Time blocked for calls and activities with her kids.
  • Orange (Friend Time): Social engagements, such as lunch with friends.
  • Purple: Growth and courses taken.
  • Dark Green: One-on-one coaching clients.
  • Dark Purple: Podcast recordings, Instagram filming, and other content creation tasks.

2. Scheduling and Booking Principles

  • Pacing: Hillary prefers no more than three back-to-back calls. Suggest rescheduling a fourth call for the next day or sending the calendar link again.
  • Clustering: Group similar tasks together.
    • Podcast Recordings: Book on Tuesdays or Thursdays.
    • Team Meetings: Schedule on Mondays and Tuesdays.
    • Fridays: Prefer fewer or no calls, flexible based on priorities.
  • Morning Routine: Protect Hillary's dedicated two-hour morning routine. Run urgent meeting requests during this block by her first.
  • Booking Window: Do not book meetings more than four weeks (one month) in advance. Ideal for connection calls is within three weeks. Urgent, high-interest connections can be booked as soon as the next day or within the week.
  • Communication: Always communicate with Hillary about her schedule. Confirm if she wants to open a new day for calls (e.g., Fridays) or take an extra meeting.

3. Travel Management

  • Travel Schedule: Note time zone changes when Hillary travels. She is typically in-office Tuesday through Thursday when traveling.
  • Calendar Itinerary: Input flight information (confirmation number, departure/arrival times) at the top of her calendar.
  • Travel Itinerary: Create a detailed travel itinerary document outlining activities and schedule flow for the entire trip. Gather info from emails or by asking Hillary/event organizer.
  • Team Calendar: Update the team calendar (Hello calendar) with Hillary’s travel schedule.

4. Team Calendar (Hello Calendar) Management

  • Add the Core Team: Always add the core team (Destiny, Kristen, etc.) to any relevant calendar event.
  • Bi-Weekly Webinars: Add to the team calendar.
  • VIP Calls: Include on the team calendar.
  • Webinars with Outside Guests: On the team calendar, especially if promoted on LinkedIn.
  • Hillary’s Speaking Opportunities: Add virtual or in-person speaking engagements to the team calendar for social media awareness/promotion.
  • Travel Schedule: Add Hillary's travel days to the team calendar.

Travel Coordination SOPs

Travel Coordination Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

1. Booking Flights

  • Airlines: Hillary primarily flies United Airlines and occasionally JSX (for California).
  • Researching Flights: Research United's website for flights departing between 8:00 AM and 11:00 AM, her preferred time (also dependent on event start time).
  • Hillary's Involvement: Send her researched flight times; she typically books herself on her United account (First Class, upgrade list if unavailable).
  • JSX: You may have account access to book directly.
  • Ground Transportation: Hillary books her own car services (e.g., Uber). She does not use airport lounges and prefers to arrive close to boarding time.

2. Booking Hotels

  • Your Responsibility: Book Hillary's hotel accommodations.
  • Preferences: Generally Marriott hotels (accrues points). For short trips, no luxury hotel required.
  • Booking Process:
    • Check with the Event Organizer: See if there's a required hotel.
    • Reach out to the Point of Contact: If no required hotel, ask for a recommendation close to the venue.
    • Special Requests: Call the hotel for special arrangements (e.g., double room for her and son, extra room for a guest).

3. Emergency Protocols

  • Primary Point of Contact: You are Hillary’s primary point of contact for schedule-related emergencies (family, health).
  • Communication: She will contact you via phone call or text.
  • Calendar Management: Immediately wipe out the rest of her calendar for the day and the first half of the following morning.
  • Rescheduling: Reschedule canceled meetings. New dates depend on the emergency and Hillary’s availability, but a good starting point is one to two weeks out.
